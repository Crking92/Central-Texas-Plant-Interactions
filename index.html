What this dashboard is, and is not

This dashboard shows verified or source-backed direct interactions from the current data package. There are more interactions in nature than shown here. A blank or low score usually means not enough verified data have been added yet, not that the plant has no ecological value.

Direct means the animal uses the plant itself: nectar, pollen, fruit, seed, leaf tissue, larval host tissue, gall/boring/mining tissue, browse, nest material, nesting substrate, cover, or roosting structure. Indirect food webs, microbes, fungi, worms, and most parasite records are intentionally left out of this public layer.

How to understand the dashboard
This is a verified-source dashboard, not a complete list of everything that happens in nature.
Plants may support more birds, mammals, bees, butterflies, moths, beetles, and other animals than shown here. The viewer only counts interactions that passed the current source and filter rules.
  • Target-zone plants come from the supplied master plant list.
  • Outside-review plants have interaction evidence but are not recommendations until native-zone validation is complete.
  • Non-native/invasive flags do not delete the interaction data; they warn users not to treat those plants as native restoration recommendations.
  • Broad guild rows such as “birds eat fruit” are shown but score lower than species-level evidence.
