What this dashboard shows

This dashboard shows verified or source-backed direct interactions from the current plant-animal dataset. There are more interactions in nature than shown here. A blank or low score usually means not enough verified data have been added yet, not that the plant has no ecological value.

Direct means the animal uses the plant itself: nectar, pollen, fruit, seed, leaf tissue, larval host tissue, gall/boring/mining tissue, browse, nest material, nesting substrate, cover, or roosting structure. Indirect food webs, microbes, fungi, worms, and most parasite records are intentionally left out of this public view.

How citations are shown

Each interaction row shows a public-facing source summary. Scores are not raw species counts: species-level, local, and better-sourced records count more; broad guild or profile records count less. Grades are tuned for the current Central Texas dataset, not a school-style 90/80/70 scale. A low or blank category usually means the current verified dataset is thin for that category, not that the plant has no ecological value.

How to read this dashboard

Direct animal-plant interactions only
Important: this dashboard is not saying these are the only animals that use each plant. It is showing interactions that are currently present in verified or source-backed records.
  • Birds, butterflies and moths, and bees are shown first in plant detail pages.
  • Scores are balanced: species-level and local records score higher, while broad guild/database records are intentionally down-weighted.
  • Non-native or invasive flags affect recommendation status, even when wildlife use is documented.
  • iNaturalist photos are shown for plant recognition only; they are not used as interaction evidence.
